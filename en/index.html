What does "4% (3+1)" mean for FB accounts?
4% is the top-up fee — top up $100 to your ad account, pay $104.
(3+1) means 3% platform fee + 1% service fee, baked into each top-up.
TK accounts follow the same logic at 5.5% (4.5+1), and TK Whitelisted accounts at 8% (7+1).
What's the difference between TK Whitelisted accounts and regular TK accounts?
TK Whitelisted accounts are TikTok platform-whitelisted accounts with several key advantages over regular TK accounts:
· Relaxed review: Can run high-risk verticals that regular accounts can't (replica/FP, weight loss/JF, finance/FN, etc.)
· Higher stability: Significantly lower ban risk compared to regular accounts
· Limited supply: Whitelist slots controlled by TikTok official agencies — restricted availability
· Higher spend cap: Daily budget limits and total scale notably higher than regular accounts
The 8% (7+1) fee reflects resource scarcity, but for advertisers running high-AOV verticals, it's a must-have account type — ROI far exceeds regular accounts.
